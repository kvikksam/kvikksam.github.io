About Us

A collection that was exclusively accessible to only medical students and practitioners for educational purposes has now been made available to high school students with the aim of engaging the students and creating a hub of functional knowledge. Our anatomical collection consists of various learning materials and artefacts for anatomical studies, using different techniques like plastination, corrosion casting, wet specimens and more for preserving what has been so kindly donated to us. We are inviting students to learn and be inspired in an extraordinary environment in a respectful manner, while challenging you to think about how we as human beings tackle the theme of death. This exhibition would not have been possible without our donors and the curators of the anatomical collection.

Born in the Netherlands, Guus Wellesen, is a prize-winning taxidermist and departmental engineer for the Department of Clinical and Molecular Medicine at NTNU, who has an essential role in the curation of the anatomical collection. Having experience with skeletal articulation since he was 7 years old, he has broad competence and Wellesen’s expertise has extended into more techniques in preserving both animals and human bodies. His attention to detail is prominent in his work, creating not only beautiful but very precise artefacts which has made this collection possible.

With a Bachelor of Technology in Photography and a Master of Fine Arts, South-African born Natalie Field is an interdisciplinary researcher and sci-artist, specializing in the field of speculative taxidermy. Field’s work explores the cultural and societal questions that are raised with how human-made representations of animals influence our behaviours towards the natural world. Field is currently a staff engineer in the Department of Clinical and Molecular Medicine at NTNU, where she is contributing with her expertise to projects that bridge the gap between human-health and environmental well-being. Field’s work has been exhibited internationally in multiple countries such as the US, Italy, Sweden and South-Africa.
